What is Pork Adobo?

Pork Adobo is the Philippines' iconic national dish, featuring tender pork (usually belly or shoulder) braised in a rich, tangy, savory sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, creating a flavorful stew often served over rice.

It's a hearty, slow-cooked comfort food known for its unique balance of salty, sour, and garlicky notes, with variations adding sugar, coconut milk, or even tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic (minced)
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorns
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Marinate the pork belly in soy sauce and garlic for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat a pot and cook the marinated pork belly for a few minutes until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the remaining marinade (including garlic).
  4. Add water, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour until pork is tender.
  5. Add vinegar and simmer for another 12 to 15 minutes.
  6. Season with salt to taste.
  7. Serve hot with freshly cooked white rice.

Tips:

  • Use pork belly for a rich, tender texture, but you can substitute with pork shoulder for a leaner version.
  • Letting the dish rest for a few hours (or overnight) enhances the flavor as the meat absorbs more of the sauce.
  • Some cooks add a pinch of sugar or onions for a slightly sweeter balance.
