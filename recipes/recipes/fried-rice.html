Fried Rice

Photo by Nadja M from Pexel

What is Fried Rice

Fried rice is a popular Asian dish of cooked rice stir-fried in a pan or wok with other ingredients like eggs, vegetables, meat, or seafood, seasoned with soy sauce, garlic, and other aromatics, often made with leftover rice to prevent waste.

It's known for its versatility, allowing for countless variations and serving as a main dish or side, with origins tracing back to China's Sui dynasty.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old for best texture)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 cup chopped white onion
  • 1 cup peas and carrots (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 lightly beaten eggs
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add onion, peas, and carrots. Cook until tender.
  3. Push veggies to the side of the pan. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble until cooked.
  4. Mix eggs with vegetables.
  5. Add rice and stir well to combine.
  6. Pour soy sauce over the rice mixture. Stir-fry until evenly coated and heated through.
  7. Garnish with green onions and serve hot.

Tips:

  • Use cold, leftover rice to avoid clumping.
  • Add proteins like chicken, shrimp, or pork for a heartier version.
  • Adjust soy sauce and seasoning to taste.
