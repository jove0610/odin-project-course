Lasagna

What is Lasagna

Lasagna is both a specific type of wide, flat pasta and the name for a popular Italian baked dish made by stacking layers of that pasta with various fillings.

The word "lasagna" originally referred to the cooking pot rather than the food itself. It is believed to stem from the Greek word lasanon (a pot stand) or the Latin lasanum (cooking pot).

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1/2 pound lean ground beef
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 36 oz pasta sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 large egg
  • 4 cups mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • 1/2 cup parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook noodles in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Brown beef, sausage, onion, and garlic in a skillet until no pink remains. Drain excess fat.
  4. Add pasta sauce, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Mix ricotta, parsley, egg, 1½ cups mozzarella, and ¼ cup parmesan in a bowl.
  6. Layer in a 9x13 pan:
    • Spread 1 cup meat sauce on the bottom.
    • Add 3 noodles.
    • Spread ⅓ of ricotta mixture.
    • Add 1 cup meat sauce.
    • Repeat layers twice more.
    • Finish with noodles topped with remaining sauce.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes.
  8. Remove foil, sprinkle remaining mozzarella and parmesan, then bake uncovered for 15 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  9. Rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Tips:

  • Use a mix of beef and sausage for depth of flavor.
  • Letting lasagna rest before cutting helps it hold its shape.
  • You can freeze assembled lasagna before baking or freeze leftovers after baking for easy future meals.
